Qwen AI hits 10m+ downloads as Aibaba disrupts the AI market

Steam Machine may target $699 price point

Steam Machine may launch at around US $699.99 — roughly in line with current consoles. Analysts and people estimating DIY‑equivalent builds suggest that due to rising RAM and SSD prices, putting together a comparable PC today would run closer to $800–$900, which makes the Steam Machine’s ~700 USD price relatively competitive. Still, there’s uncertainty: some expect higher pricing (especially for better‑spec models), and a few forecasts put the cost anywhere between mid‑$500s to up to $1,000+ depending on components and configuration. Bottom line — Steam Machine is likely aiming to sit between console and PC pricing, offering a living‑room PC‑style gaming experience without breaking the bank (comparatively).

2025-10-29

Nvidia reveals Vera Rubin Superchip for the first time

NVIDIA has unveiled its Vera Rubin Superchip, a high-density board combining an 88-core Vera CPU, two Rubin GPUs, and eight SOCAMM memory modules. Designed for AI and data-center workloads, the compact board delivers extreme compute and memory bandwidth, with each GPU capable of massive FP4 performance and high-speed NVLink-C2C connectivity. NVIDIA plans to begin production in 2026, with deployments expected by late 2026 or early 2027.

2025-11-11

AMD reveals new roadmap for its Ryzen CPUs, teasing Zen 7 as the true "next-generation" leap

Zen 6 is officially confirmed for release around 2026 — built on TSMC’s 2 nm process, with much better efficiency, higher IPC (instructions‑per‑clock), and expanded AI support across desktop (Ryzen), mobile, and server (EPYC) chips. Looking further ahead, Zen 7 gets its first official mention — slated as the “true next‑generation” leap, possibly arriving after 2026 on a yet‑undisclosed process node. It’s expected to bring a new “Matrix Engine” and enhanced AI‑native features that could redefine AMD’s CPU lineup.

This is why memory and storage is so expensive (of course it's AI) and why PC gaming hardware prices are only going to keep rising, even probably for GPUs

RAM and storage prices are surging worldwide due to booming AI and data‑center demand, with major manufacturers like Samsung and SK Hynix raising costs by up to 30%. Why The AI Bubble Isn't Likely Popping Any Time Soon

Despite recent fluctuations in AI-related stocks, experts argue that the AI growth cycle is far from over. Robust earnings forecasts for leading AI companies, continued investment in infrastructure, and favorable macroeconomic conditions—such as potential interest rate cuts and government AI initiatives—support ongoing expansion. Analysts suggest this combination could drive sustained momentum in the sector, with major market indices potentially reaching new highs through the end of 2025 and into 2026.
